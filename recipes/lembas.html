Lembas Bread

Get ready for your travels through middle earth with this sustaining food

Ingredients

  • 6 Tablespoons butter, slightly softened, still cool
  • 2 Cups self rising flour
  • 1 Tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 Egg, well beaten
  • 1/2 cup + 1 Tablespoon of heavy cream
  • Mallorn leaves and twine or raffia (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees.
  2. Cut softened butter into the flour using a pastry blender until it has a crumbly consistency.
  3. Add brown sugar, cinnamon, and freshly grated nutmeg, mix.
  4. Beat the egg well in a small bowl, then add heavy cream and mix.
  5. Take 1 Tablespoon of the egg mixture and set aside.
  6. Add the egg mixture to the flour mixture and mix until it forms a stiff, soft dough. Do not overmix.
  7. Dump the mixture onto a lightly floured surface and knead 3-4 times.
  8. Roll dough to approximately 3/4" thick. Cut into 3 inch squares and transfer to a lightly greased cookie sheet, leaving about an inch of space between each square. Score the dough with a butter knife (do not cut all the way through) from corner to corner in both directions, forming an "X" on the dough. (See "note" below)
  9. Brush the tops of the squares with the reserved egg mixture.
  10. Bake for 12-13 minutes until it is set and lightly golden.
  11. If using mallorn leaves, allow lembas to cool and then wrap in mallorn leaves, securing with twine or raffia.

Note: If properly scored, lembas squares should break apart easily into 4 triangles.
